January 2019

The Integrated Concentration of Science (iCons) Biodigester Team is founded by Joshue McGee and Hansen Tjo.

May 2019

The iCons Biodigester Team wins 1st Place at the 2019 iCons Annual Energy Debate.

April 2020

The iCons Biodigester Team wins the $2100 Sustainability Innovation & Engagement Fund (SIEF) Grant. The SIEF grant is awarded to innovative projects that provide cost savings or engages the community to become more sustainability-oriented. This money will be used to purchase equipment for constructing the lab-scale biodigester.

September 2020

The iCons Biodigester Team delivers outreach presentation on their work to Introduction to Chemical Engineering students.

December 2020

Anaerobic Digester website is published and planning for lab scale biodigester begins.

February 2021

The iCons Biodigester Team delivers outreach presentation on their work to iCons underclassmen.

September 2021

The iCons Biodigester Team officially rebrands into Undergradates Raising Awareness for Anaerobic Digestion (URAAD) and website is renovated.

January 2022

Lab scale biodigester planning finishes and construction begins while undergraduate thesis begins to improve feedstock component of URAAD website.

April 2022

The URAAD Team Wins [Honorable Mention](https://www.umass.edu/news/article/umass-amherst-libraries-announce-winners-undergraduate-sustainability-awards) at the 2022 Undergraduate Sustainability Awards Competition!
